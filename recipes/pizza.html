Pizza

Description

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve sugar in warm water. Sprinkle yeast over the top, and let stand for about 10 minutes, until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center, and pour in yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix well using a wooden spoon, then turn out onto a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 10 minutes. Shape into a ball. Place dough in a well oiled bowl, and turn dough to coat. Cover with a damp cloth. Allow to rise until doubled in bulk, about 1 hour.
  3. Punch dough down. Knead for a few minutes, and divide in half. Shape into two balls. Place on a nonstick baking sheet, and cover with a damp cloth. Allow to rise until doubled.
  4. Preheat oven to 475 degrees F (245 degrees C). Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the pizza crust. Add grated cheese, and desired toppings. Bake in preheated oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until golden brown.
